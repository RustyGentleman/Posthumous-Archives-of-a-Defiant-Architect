# Broad Strokes

Though I've built this world _"from the inside out"_, starting from a character, I do have some idea of how it looks and works at a larger scale.

Being who I am, and what I am, part of my struggles and stubbornness is __willing that this world make sense__. It must be internally consistent, and, in a lot of ways, that falls back to it also being consistent with reality, wherever I haven't willed it otherwise. As such, it does consist of a planet, which does orbit a star, and it does possess a natural satellite.

<img id="rasmaincontinent" src="assets/ras_maincontinent.png"/>

The "main" continent, from what I've resolved, looks _vaguely_ like the image above. It's placed on the northern hemisphere, covers the north pole, and spans nearly all the way to the equator at a slight angle. The western side of the main continent consists mostly of silverwood and old growth forests, grasslands and the like, with a drier climate compared to the eastern side, where the north is dominated by boreal and mixed temperate forests. Towards the south, there's a band of rocky elevated terrain, separating the extreme south of the continent from its center. That extreme south consists mostly of jungles and wetlands, especially towards the east.

There is another continent to the east that spans further south over the equator, with its closest point to the main continent being a western desertic peninsula near the equator. That peninsula and the surrounding region would, in the past, slot up against the main continent's southeastern jungles and wetlands. A region of rocky, craggy terrain separates the peninsula from further inland, where I'm sure there are greener, more savanna-like regions, though that hasn't been resolved further than that.

There is also a third continent towards the southwest of the main continent, though it is a bit special. All but a relatively thin sliver that spans along its eastern, southeastern, southern, and western shores, is characterized by very volcanically active terrain and odd rocky formations, has relatively little soil, and is marred by the long-lasting aftereffects of what is theorized to have been some form of natural magical catastrophe. The totality of these aftereffects and the various weird phenomena they cause are referred to as _'the scar'_, though the region itself is often also referred to as such.

# Populations

The main continent is quite well populated, with the main elf populations occupying the western and midwestern regions, elfins occupying... most of the continent, frankly; wolfkins occupying mostly the more inland regions, a dozen or so pockets of native kitsune populations, harpies occupying the more mountainous regions and those with significant elevation changes, and marshland lizans mostly contained to the southeastern wetlands. Of course, that is a general rule; I'm sure _a few_ marshland lizans must live in elven communities along the western side of the continent, and I can very well imagine there might be a few groups of nomadic wolfkins that have roamed the marshlands.

In the eastern continent, I know there are desert lizans in... y'know, _the desert_ peninsula, and the surrounding regions. I know somewhere there are felikins - something between a wolfkin and a kitsune, with feline traits - because one lives in Crestbend Harbor. I'm sure there must be some variants of wolfkins, kitsunes and harpies, as well.

The 'scarred continent' is devoid of truly sapient beings, though I'm sure there are some very intelligent animals there.

# Cosmology

This world I speak of is one of many that exist within a cosmological deity of sorts, with its own personality and character. It is not aware that these worlds exist, but it does affect them. I had given it a name, but I no longer remember it; however, I remember that it too is autistic, and that causes the worlds that ebb and flow in and out of existence within it tend to be relatively stable and sensical.
