# On Time and Suffering

...I can't say that I'd always suffered. That would be dishonest and disingenuous on my part. I had moments of joy, and moments of true happiness, albeit... frustratingly less and fewer than others seem to have had by that point in their lives. But suffering had always been a constant in my life. Especially since I was diagnosed with chronic depression.

I had always prided myself as being of strong mind and willpower. I could brave through the lows in my depression by hunkering down and shutting off, waiting for it to pass, and I'd appear on the other side. But I was always waiting for the next time. And a next time always came. Year after year, through every era and phase and every important moment in my life, it always loomed over me; that uncaring, indifferent dark mass that would rob me of so much, with no ill intent, no rhyme or reason.

Time... wore me. As it does everything. Time eats away at memories, at meaning. And it ate away at my resolve. Even when I didn't carry it alone... it was no less certain. No one could ease the burden of certainty; of knowing how likely it was that I would have to _endure it_ for the rest of my life. All who were close to me were familiar of how deathly afraid I was of that. And that certainty grew with every year I carried on. Time ate away, too, at my chances of being rid of this cruel thing.

It was a great battle. I scrambled to find meaning in things, to give myself good reasons to keep on living; to cultivate them just to keep up with the pain of certainty.
